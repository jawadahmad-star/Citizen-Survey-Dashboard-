Survey at a Glance

Real-time monitoring of the Citizen Property Tax & RS Assessment Survey across Lahore. The study measures how property owners perceive the Excise & Taxation Department, how much they trust satellite / remote-sensing (RS) based property valuation, and what they would pay for their own RS estimate — tracked against the 2,127-property revised sampling frame across E&T circles and localities.

Willingness to Pay for an RS Estimate

Would citizens pay for their own satellite-based property estimate? Each respondent is offered the product at a randomised price (Rs 0 / 100 / 200) — tracing out a real demand curve — after completing an 11-round BACE choice experiment over hypothetical assessment options.

Demand Curve — Uptake by Randomised Price

% of respondents who accept the offer to buy their own RS estimate, at each randomly assigned price

Would Buy Own RS Estimate

Overall uptake across all price points

Current Assessed Tax Liability

Annual property-tax liability of sampled properties (prefilled)

Believability of BACE Scenarios (0–10)

How believable respondents found the hypothetical valuation options

Prices are randomised at the property level in the sampling frame, so uptake differences across price points identify the demand curve. BACE = Bayesian Adaptive Choice Experiment (11 adaptive rounds per respondent).

🏠 Progress by Property Land Size (marla)

Each property in the sampling frame is binned by its recorded land area (marla). Target = sampled properties in that band; completed = interviews done in that band. Helps track whether smaller vs larger properties are being covered evenly.

Locality Land Size E&T Circle Completed Sampled Target Completion ▼ Status

Each row is a locality × land-size band (property land area in marla). Targets = sampled properties in that band; completion % = completed ÷ target. Rows are grouped by locality (bands smallest-first) by default — click any column to re-sort, or use the Land Size filter to isolate one band.
